In this setting, darshan means coming before Mother Meera in person and receiving her blessing in silence. It is not a talk, a class, or a public performance; it is a brief individual encounter that takes place within a quiet, orderly gathering.

What happens is usually simple and structured. People wait until their turn, approach one by one, sit or kneel briefly before Mother Meera, and then move on so the line can continue without interruption.

The expectation is to follow the local instructions, keep the atmosphere quiet, and arrive ready for a short encounter rather than a conversation. Visitors do not need prior knowledge, but they should understand that darshan is centered on silence, attention, and the shared order of the gathering.
